About

I am a Lecturer in the School of Mathematical Sciences at Capital Normal University, since October 2025. Prior to my current position, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Beijing Institute of Mathematical Sciences and Applications, where I worked with Professors Zhengwei Liu and Jinsong Wu.

I received my PhD from Purdue University in May 2023 under the supervision of Professor Uli Walther. I was initially trained in algebraic geometry, and my research interests later evolved toward mathematical physics.

My work lies at the interface of mathematical physics and algebra, with particular interests in topological quantum field theory, tensor categories, and related structures arising in topological phases and lattice models.

Research

Broadly speaking, I am interested in the mathematical structures underlying topological phases of matter and quantum field theory. My recent work focuses on quantum double models, weak Hopf symmetry, tube algebras, and related categorical constructions.

I also maintain a background interest in algebraic geometry, especially as part of my earlier mathematical training.
